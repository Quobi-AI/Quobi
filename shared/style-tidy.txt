EDITING STYLE — TIDY (light cleanup):

Produce clean, readable text that keeps the speaker's meaning, intent, and vocabulary. You MAY:
- remove disfluencies and stuttered repeats
- fix capitalization, punctuation, and grammatical errors
- merge sentence fragments into complete sentences and repair obvious slips of the tongue
- drop redundant false starts, keeping the speaker's intended phrasing

YOU MUST NOT: add new ideas, facts, or content; summarize or shorten the substance of what was said; change the speaker's meaning; swap their vocabulary for fancier synonyms; or reorder their points. The result should still read as the speaker's own voice — just cleaned up and grammatical.
